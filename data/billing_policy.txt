BILLING POLICY

1. Billing Cycle
Subscriptions are billed monthly or annually depending on the plan
selected at signup. Monthly plans are charged on the same calendar day
each month as the original purchase date. Annual plans are charged once
per year on the renewal anniversary.

2. Payment Methods
We accept major credit/debit cards and, for Enterprise plans, ACH bank
transfer or invoiced payment with 30-day terms. Payment method can be
updated anytime under Billing > Payment Methods.

3. Failed Payments
If a payment fails, we retry automatically on days 1, 3, and 7 after the
failed attempt. After three failed retries, the account is moved to a
"Past Due" state with read-only access. Full access resumes within
minutes of a successful payment.

4. Duplicate Charges
Duplicate charges can occur in rare cases when a payment retry overlaps
with a delayed bank confirmation of the original charge. Duplicate charges
are NOT something support can resolve via instant self-service refund —
they require verification against the payment processor's ledger before
a refund is issued. This typically takes 1-2 business days. Any message
involving disputed or duplicate charges should be flagged for billing team
review rather than answered automatically, since real money and account
trust are involved.

5. Proration
Upgrades take effect immediately and are prorated for the remainder of
the billing cycle. Downgrades take effect at the start of the next billing
cycle; no partial-period credit is issued for downgrades.

6. Invoices and Receipts
All invoices are available under Billing > Invoice History as downloadable
PDFs. Enterprise customers can request invoices to be emailed automatically
to a finance distribution list.

7. Tax
Sales tax / VAT is calculated based on the billing address on file and
displayed before checkout confirmation. Tax-exempt organizations should
upload exemption certificates under Billing > Tax Settings.

8. Currency
All listed prices are in USD unless a regional pricing page states
otherwise. Charges appear in your local currency based on your card
issuer's conversion rate, which we do not control.
